Disease Sim.

What is Disease Sim?

Disease Sim is a tool to help you simulate disease spread. By setting the parameters, you can watch how the epidemic will unfold.

The States

The states used in the simulation are:
  • Healthy - someone who has never had the disease and is susceptible.
  • Asymptomatic - someone who is infected, but doesn't show symptoms, and will social distance less than someone who does show symptoms.
  • Infected - someone who is infected and shows symptoms.
  • Severely Infected - someone who is infected and who's condition is very bad. Severely infected people will go to the hospital if there is room.
  • Recovered - someone who had the disease, but no longer does. Recovered people cannot become infected again.
  • Dead - someone who has died from the disease.

The Parameters

The parameters are:
  • Population size - the total number of people in the simulation.
  • Initial infected population - how many people will start out with the disease.
  • Infection radius - the minimum distance two people can be from each other before the disease can spread between them.
    This parameter is relative to the size of a person. E.g. a value of 3 means the distance two people can be apart before the disease can spread between them is equivalent to the size of 3 people.
  • Infection rate - the probability that the disease can spread between two people every day.
  • Recovery rate - the probability that an infected person will recover every day.
  • Mortality rate - the probability that an infected person will die every day.
  • Hospital capacity - the maximum number of people that can be in the hospital.
  • Hospital recovery rate - the probability that someone in the hospital will recover every day.
  • Hospital mortality rate - the probability that someone in the hospital will die every day.
  • Social distance radius - the minimum distance two people can be from each other before they will begin to socially distance from each other.
    This parameter is relative to the size of a person. E.g. a value of 3 means the distance two people can be apart before they socially distance from each other is equivalent to the size of 3 people.
  • Asymptomatic period - the number of days a person is asymptomatic for.
  • Infectious period - the maximum number of days a person can be infected for
